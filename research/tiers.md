# Maturity Tiers

The five tiers represent distinct organizational AI maturity levels. Each tier has a characteristic profile and set of recommended next steps.

---

## Einsteiger (Score: 1.0–1.8)

**Profile:** AI is on the radar but not in the room. Individual experiments exist, but there is no strategy, no budget, and no ownership. Data is scattered. Most employees are skeptical or unaware.

**Typical quotes from this tier:**
- "We're looking into ChatGPT for some use cases."
- "IT is running a pilot, but leadership isn't really involved."
- "We don't have a data strategy yet."

**Priority actions:**
1. Executive alignment session — AI strategy can't come from IT alone
2. Quick-win use case: pick one high-visibility, low-risk AI application and ship it
3. Data audit: where is your data, who owns it, is it accessible?

---

## Erkunder (Score: 1.8–2.6)

**Profile:** First experiments are running. There's enthusiasm in pockets of the organization, but no coherent strategy. Data infrastructure is being built. Skills are emerging but unevenly distributed.

**Typical profile:**
- 1–3 AI pilots, some positive results
- Strong individual champions, weak institutional support
- Data teams exist but are siloed

**Priority actions:**
1. Connect pilots to strategy — which use cases actually map to business priorities?
2. Build a small, cross-functional AI task force
3. Start upskilling: all department heads should understand AI basics

---

## Gestalter (Score: 2.6–3.4)

**Profile:** AI is becoming part of how the organization operates. Strategy exists, there's budget, and leadership is engaged. Some production deployments. The challenge is scaling and governance.

**Typical profile:**
- 3–10 live AI use cases
- Dedicated AI team or function
- First governance questions emerging (ethics, compliance, data privacy)

**Priority actions:**
1. Build the AI governance framework before scaling further
2. Systematize: create a repeatable process for evaluating, building, and deploying AI use cases
3. Address the Readiness Gap if present — alignment between leadership and employees is critical at this stage

---

## Umsetzer (Score: 3.4–4.2)

**Profile:** AI is a competitive advantage. Multiple production deployments, measurable ROI, and a strong internal capability. The organization learns fast and iterates. Culture has shifted.

**Typical profile:**
- AI embedded in core processes
- Strong data infrastructure
- AI fluency across most functions

**Priority actions:**
1. Invest in proprietary data assets and fine-tuned models
2. Start thinking about agentic AI — autonomous systems that act, not just generate
3. Contribute to external AI governance and standards (industry groups, EU AI Act compliance as opportunity)

---

## Vorreiter (Score: 4.2–5.0)

**Profile:** AI is foundational. The organization doesn't just use AI — it builds with it, learns from it, and is beginning to redefine its industry with it. Speed of learning is a core competency.

**Typical profile:**
- AI-first in product, operations, and strategy
- Proprietary models or significant fine-tuning
- Active role in shaping AI policy and standards

**Priority actions:**
1. Define the next frontier: what does AI-native mean for your industry in 5 years?
2. Ecosystem thinking — your AI advantage is your network and data flywheel
3. Talent: at this level, you're competing with the hyperscalers for talent
