# AI Readiness Framework

## Overview

The assessment measures organizational AI readiness across **7 dimensions**. Each dimension is independently scored on a 1–5 scale and contributes to a weighted total score.

---

## The 7 Dimensions

### D1 — KI-Strategie & Leadership (Weight: 18%)
Does leadership have a clear AI vision? Is AI embedded in corporate strategy, not just experimented with in silos?

Key aspects:
- Strategic anchoring of AI at board level
- Dedicated AI budget and resources
- Clear ownership and accountability
- Link between AI initiatives and business goals

### D2 — Datenfundament & Infrastruktur (Weight: 18%)
Is the data foundation ready for AI? Quality, availability, and governance of data.

Key aspects:
- Data quality and accessibility
- Data infrastructure (cloud, pipelines, warehouses)
- Data governance and ownership
- Real-time vs. batch data availability

### D3 — KI-Kompetenz & Talent (Weight: 18%)
Does the organization have the skills to build, buy, and use AI?

Key aspects:
- Internal AI/ML expertise
- Upskilling programs for non-technical staff
- Ability to evaluate external AI vendors
- AI literacy across the organization

### D4 — Unternehmenskultur & Change (Weight: 12%)
Is the culture ready for AI-driven change? Are employees open or resistant?

Key aspects:
- Psychological safety around AI experimentation
- Leadership modeling AI adoption
- Handling of AI-related job concerns
- Speed of change adoption

### D5 — Prozesse & Governance (Weight: 12%)
Are there processes for responsible AI deployment? Legal, ethical, and operational guardrails.

Key aspects:
- AI ethics guidelines and policies
- Risk assessment processes for AI use cases
- Compliance with EU AI Act / GDPR
- Escalation paths for AI failures

### D6 — KI-Umsetzung & Use Cases (Weight: 8%)
Has the organization actually implemented AI? Pilot projects, production deployments, measurable ROI.

Key aspects:
- Number and quality of live AI use cases
- ROI measurement of AI initiatives
- Speed from idea to deployment
- Learnings from past AI projects

### D7 — Digitale Grundkompetenz (Weight: 14%)
Do employees have the foundational digital skills to work alongside AI tools?

Key aspects:
- Basic digital tool proficiency (beyond Office)
- Comfort with automation tools
- Ability to prompt and work with AI assistants
- Digital self-learning capability

---

## Scoring

**Dimension score** = average of question responses within dimension (scale 1–5)

**Total score** = weighted sum of all dimension scores

```
totalScore = Σ (dimensionScore_i × weight_i)
```

**Tier assignment** based on total score:

| Score Range | Tier |
|---|---|
| 1.0 – < 1.8 | Einsteiger |
| 1.8 – < 2.6 | Erkunder |
| 2.6 – < 3.4 | Gestalter |
| 3.4 – < 4.2 | Umsetzer |
| 4.2 – 5.0 | Vorreiter |

---

## Special Scores

**CXO Score** — Average of leadership/strategic questions (F1, F2, F3, F6, F7, F16, F20, F21, F23, F24, F25)

**Employee Score** — Average of operational/people questions

**Readiness Gap** = |CXO Score − Employee Score|

A high Readiness Gap (>0.8) indicates misalignment between leadership perception and ground-level reality — often the most actionable finding.

---

## The T-Score (Transformation Score)

A separate, parallel score (not included in totalScore) that measures whether the organization views AI as:
- A **tool** (automation of existing work)
- A **fundamental transformation** (reimagining how the business operates)

Scale: Werkzeug → Optimierer → Integrierer → Transformierer → Neue Wirtschaft

This score is deliberately kept separate because organizations with identical readiness scores can have fundamentally different AI trajectories depending on their transformation mindset.
